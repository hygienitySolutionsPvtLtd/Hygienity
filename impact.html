Operational impact for sanitation systems should be measurable, not anecdotal.

Hygienity is focused on outcomes that matter to infrastructure owners: lower water intensity, reduced cleaning frequency, lower maintenance costs, and a more sustainable hygiene model. All figures on this page are directional and based on pilot observations rather than final commercial claims.

Water savings

Less rinsing pressure

Cleaner surface behavior may reduce the need for repeated washdowns and aggressive flushing during routine upkeep.

Cost reduction

Optimized maintenance

Lower labor intensity, fewer heavy-clean interventions, and more efficient chemical use can improve cost predictability.

Cleaning frequency

Reduced deep-clean events

The coating is intended to help slow the buildup that typically forces high-effort cleaning cycles.

Environmental benefit

Less resource intensity

Potentially lower water and chemical demand supports a more sustainable sanitation footprint.

Environmental Benefits

By reducing the frequency and intensity of cleaning, preventive coatings may help cut water demand, lower dependence on strong chemicals, and improve the efficiency of sanitation operations across large asset networks.

Investor Relevance

The opportunity sits at the intersection of materials science, infrastructure efficiency, public health, and sustainability, with a clear path to measurable operating outcomes if pilots continue to validate the approach.
